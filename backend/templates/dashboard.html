Consent & Setup

Before tracking starts, please review what this system tracks, how it behaves, and how your data will be used.

System purpose

Digital Behaviour Twin is an academic productivity tracking system. It observes your activity patterns and turns them into dashboards, goals, focus analytics, reports, alerts, and behaviour insights.

What it can track

  • App names or browser window titles from connected tracking devices
  • Usage duration, timestamps, device identifiers, and sync events
  • OCR text snapshots when OCR verification is enabled
  • Goal settings, focus metrics, streaks, reports, and risk scores
  • Connected device details such as laptop, browser, phone, and online status

How the system behaves

  • It classifies activity into productive, distracting, or neutral categories
  • It updates your dashboard and predictions automatically from stored activity data
  • It may trigger alerts, summaries, and blocker actions if those features are enabled
  • It stores the above tracking data in the project database for analysis and reporting

Your acknowledgment

By accepting, you confirm that you understand the system behaviour, consent to your activity data being recorded for this project, and allow the stored data to be used for analytics, insights, alerts, and reports inside this application.

How It Works

1
tracker.pyw checks your risk score every 30 seconds via the heartbeat loop.
2
When risk ≥ threshold, it writes 127.0.0.1 instagram.com into your Windows hosts file.
3
Your browser can no longer reach blocked domains — they show a connection error.
4
Once risk drops below threshold, rules are automatically removed and sites unblock.
⚠️ tracker.pyw must be running as Administrator for hosts file access.
